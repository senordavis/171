Objectives

After successful completion of this assignment, the student will be able to:

  • Calculate correct scanning resolutions for different image applications.
  • Execute a series of image scans that demonstrate an appropriate tonal range.

Assignment Specifications

Using your own photographic images (prints, magazines, etc.) complete the following image scans using a flatbed scanner. Save your image scans with appropriate names, following the file naming convention:


  yourname-scan-color.psd

  yourname-scan-gray.psd

  yourname-scan-color-web.psd

Once scanned and named, upload your images to the Scanning Assignment Dropbox.

Please make sure to upload your files as PSDs. If your file is to large, please upload your PSD to the LCC Google Drive and share a link to the file with me. Then upload a JPG to the D2L Dropbox for this assignment.

If you are having problems please view the Upload guide or email me.

1. Scanning from a Color Print

For use in a high-quality 4-color printed publication.

  • Final image size: 5” wide by 3.5” high
  • Final image resolution: scan at 266 dpi
    (Normally you would change this to CMYK, but leave as RGB for this exercise)

2. Scanning from a Black and White Print

For use in a Greyscale newspaper publication.

  • Final image size: 6” wide by 4.5” high
  • Final image resolution: scan at 170 dpi
    (If you scan in RGB, be sure to convert to Grayscale before saving)

3. Scanning from a Color Print

For use in a web design.

  • Final image size: 4.5” wide by 3” high
  • Final image resolution: scan at 72 dpi
