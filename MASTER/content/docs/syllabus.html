Course Description

ARTS 171 is a hands-on course utilizing Apple Macintosh computers to provide the student with an introductory understanding of Adobe Photoshop CC image manipulation software. The course will expose students to computer manipulation of a variety of images, scanning, resolution, and color issues. Basic design skills and foundational color knowledge are necessary for enrollment.

Prerequisites

Minimum 2.5 in (ARTS 102 or PHOT 118) or Concurrently and Reading Level 5 and Writing Level 6.

Course Note: Adobe Photoshop software is used. Online students are required to have semester-long access to Adobe Photoshop CS6 or CC and a flatbed scanner to complete their assignments. Students have access to these in the LCC TLC 100 Computer Lab, or in the Computer Graphics Labs in the Gannon Building.

Outcomes

Learning Outcomes

(DEPARTMENT SPECIFIED STUDENT PERFORMANCE OBJECTIVES)

Based upon industry standards and professional practices, the successful student will be able to:

  1. Calculate scanning resolution utilizing the scanning formula and produce a digital scan with an appropriate tonal range and color mode from continuous tone photographic prints.
  2. Demonstrate an understanding of digital file characteristics acquired with a digital camera.
  3. Identify by name and use the tools from the Photoshop tool palette according to the printed Adobe Photoshop documentation.
  4. Locate, identify, and record the image mode, file size, resolution, and pixel dimensions of a variety of raster-based digital image files.
  5. Write correct definitions of commonly used digital image files
  6. Interpret and apply specific image manipulations to digital image files.
  7. Understand and manipulate digital images in various color modes for appropriate use
  8. Utilize a variety of Photoshop tools to create selections in a digital image.
  9. Select and use Photoshop retouching tools and techniques to eliminate image flaws such as scratches, dust sports, and other imperfections.
  10. Utilize the image adjustment commands in order to produce an image that demonstrates an improved histogram according to the definitions presented in ARTS 171 course materials.
  11. Utilize selections, layers, effects, and masking tools to create a finished composite image that contains elements from several original image files.
  12. Print a Photoshop document in accurate color
  13. Demonstrate appropriate use of file formats, sizes and compression for designated output.

Evaluation Components

Assignments

Assignments are in-depth, self-directed assignments used to explore and execute a non-academic design problem or series of problems. Significant research and development is required. To succeed, combine learned mechanics and principles, review and expand upon content and individual artistic expression.

You will create your own artwork responding to your research and learned skills with creative freedom.

There are 13 assignments for this course worth 10 points each. The final project is worth 30 points. Each project will expand the student’s knowledge of an important aspect of Adobe Photoshop. Projects are graded on application of subject matter, appropriate use of the software and creativity.

Plagiarism is a cardinal subject in art. Fair use will be covered in some detail throughout the class. Failing to understand fair use — intentionally or not — can cost a creative company or individual millions of dollars. This is why LCC’s goal is to correct plagiarism and unfair use early on.

Plagiarized assignments will be scored as zero with no chance to make up the assignment—whether the cause was malice or ignorance. You will lose all participation points for the day. In certain cases, particularly multiple offenses, it may be reported to the LCC Behavior Intervention Team.
